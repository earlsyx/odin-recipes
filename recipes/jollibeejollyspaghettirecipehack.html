Jollibee Jolly Spaghetti Recipe

Jollibee Jolly Spaghetti

This has to be one of your childhood favorites! The Jolly Spaghetti from Jollibee is just as delicious and nostalgic as the crispy fried chicken, and we’re not surprised: the dish has everything we want in an indulgent kiddie spaghetti recipe.

What is amazing is that it’s not just for kids. Not everyone loves spaghetti but it’s undoubtedly the spaghetti the kid inside of us will love.

If you want to relive a childhood memory or two with a Jollibee-inspired spaghetti at home, we have good news for you: Francis Lim of Tipple and Slaw and Nav came up with a recipe close to that same spaghetti that you can replicate at home.

The result of his many recipe tests and trials is the recipe you will find below. You might be surprised to see a couple of ingredients in there, but they’re what Chef Francis discovered to be necessary for creating the iconic sauce that has captivated many kids — and kids at heart — for generations.

Such ingredients include liver spread and banana ketchup which aren’t exactly new to any spaghetti. But condensed milk? We, too, were surprised. So here it is. We don’t want you to stop patronizing the happy bee just yet, but here’s a chance for you to enjoy the spaghetti any time and anywhere.

Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1 tablespoon finely chopped garlic
  • 1 tablespoon finely chopped white onion
  • 50 grams ground beef
  • 200 grams sweet Filipino-style spaghetti sauce
  • 30 grams liver spread
  • ½ cup banana ketchup
  • 50 grams sliced red hotdogs
  • ¼ cup condensed milk
  • cooked spaghetti noodles and grated cheese to serve

    • Steps in creating the dish

    1. Heat oil in a saucepan over medium heat. Saute garlic and onion until soft.
    2. Add beef and sauté until cooked.
    3. Add spaghetti sauce, banana ketchup, liver spread, hotdog slices, and condensed milk. Simmer for 5 to 10 minutes.
    4. Serve over spaghetti noodles and top with grated cheese.
